# Downloaded Papers

1. [Bullying the Machine: How Personas Increase LLM Vulnerability](2505.12692_Bullying_the_Machine.pdf)
   - Authors: Ziwei Xu, Udit Sanghi, Mohan Kankanhalli
   - Year: 2025
   - arXiv: 2505.12692
   - Why relevant: Investigates psychological pressure (bullying) and persona-driven vulnerabilities in LLMs.

2. [PADBen: A Comprehensive Benchmark for Evaluating AI Text Detectors Against Paraphrase Attacks](2511.00416_PADBen.pdf)
   - Authors: Yiwei Zha, Rui Min, Shanu Sushmita
   - Year: 2025
   - arXiv: 2511.00416
   - Why relevant: Mentions iterative paraphrasing and the "intermediate laundering region" where AI text becomes harder to detect.

3. [Large Language Models can be Guided to Evade AI-Generated Text Detection](2305.10847_SICO.pdf)
   - Authors: Ning Lu, Shengcai Liu, Ruidan He, Ke Tang
   - Year: 2023
   - arXiv: 2305.10847
   - Why relevant: Proposes SICO, a method to optimize prompts for evading AI detection via in-context learning.

4. [Detection Avoidance Techniques for Large Language Models](2503.07595_Detection_Avoidance.pdf)
   - Authors: Sinclair Schneider, Florian Steuber, João A. G. Schneider, Gabi Dreo Rodosek
   - Year: 2025
   - arXiv: 2503.07595
   - Why relevant: Demonstrates rephrasing leads to >90% evasion of zero-shot detectors like DetectGPT.

5. [Self-Disguise Attack: Induce the LLM to disguise itself for AIGT detection evasion](2412.19437_Self_Disguise_Attack.pdf)
   - Authors: Yinghan Zhou, Juan Wen, Wanli Peng, Zhengxian Wu, Ziwei Zhang, Yiming Xue
   - Year: 2024
   - arXiv: 2412.19437
   - Why relevant: Guides the LLM to actively disguise its output during generation to bypass detectors.
